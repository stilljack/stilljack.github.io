This is a fight that the good guys might actually be able to win....

Over the last couple days a situation has developed around a professional level player of Blizzard's megahit internet based collectible card game Hearthstone. Chung “Blitzchung” Ng Wai, during the broadcast of an in real life hearthstone event voiced support for hong kong protestors (which incidentally is a vote for the most core of American values: Liberty.) -- he was promptly banned from hearthstone for the length or a year and had recent tournament winnings stripped from him. Worse still, in blizzards cowardice, they also fired the two streamer personalities who were interviewing Ng Wai despite their own cowardice in, quote, "physically ducking [away] from Ng Wai"

It is not at all hard to understand this choice on blizzards part. If China feels blizzard is not doing their part to prevent dissident speech, China will not hesitate to completely block Hearthstine within Chinas borders. While this makes sense economically, it is obvioisly a vile, pathetic and cynical choice. You would be hard pressed to find anyone in this world outside of tHe PRC --and even then, outside of the communist party -- who would think this was a just and good stance for blizzard to take.

So why does this matter? This is a fight the good guys could potentially win and one that will have an impact on other companies in america and the west. If blizzard can be shown that we will stop using their products if they actively do harm to our core value of Liberty: it is possible they will back down, reinstate Ng Wai and the streamers and accept that its better to lose the chinese market than the entire western world. If on the other hand the boycott doesn't move forward we will keep seeing this sort of horrible behavior from american companies and soon, China will actively be able to enforce its draconian antispeech laws beyond its borders; and we will all watch our tongues about the PRC.

Boycot blizzard, perhaps more importantly -- spread around to anyone you can what has happened here. We can't let libetry die, now's the chance to fight this fight before this kind of behavior starts spreading and we begin to see Xi's influence engulf the west.

Tell everyone.

SharedPreferences
SharedPrefs are wild insecure and seem to really be best employed only for the most trivial of saved data. KeyStore seems interesting as an alternative that can provide at least some protection of user data and perhaps a place to store anything of more value than darktheme/False(https://developer.android.com/training/articles/keystore)
The better the model, the easier it will be to code for, everything comes out of the model.
